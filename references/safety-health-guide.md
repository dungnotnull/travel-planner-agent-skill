## HEALTH RISKS BY REGION

### Southeast Asia (Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Cambodia, Philippines)

**Water & Food Safety:** ⚠️ HIGH RISK
- NEVER drink tap water. Bottled water only — check seal is unbroken.
- Ice from restaurants is usually made from filtered water — acceptable. Ice from convenience store drinks: fine.
- Street food: safe at busy, high-turnover stalls (hot food = low risk). Avoid pre-cut fruit in the sun.
- Shellfish: moderate risk outside major cities — limit unless at reputable restaurants.

**Insect-Borne Diseases:** ⚠️ DENGUE RISK
- Dengue fever: no vaccine widely available. Mosquitoes bite day AND night.
- Use DEET 20%+ repellent, applied every 3–4 hours.
- Wear long sleeves at dawn/dusk in rural areas.
- Malaria: mainly rural/border areas. Take prophylaxis if visiting Mekong Delta, rural Cambodia, rural Myanmar.
- Zika: present; risk to pregnant travelers — consult doctor before travel.

**Vaccinations Recommended:**
- Hepatitis A (food/water borne — highly recommended)
- Typhoid (if eating local food — highly recommended)
- Hepatitis B (if any medical procedures or risk behavior)
- Japanese Encephalitis (if rural areas or stays > 4 weeks)
- Rabies pre-exposure (if working with animals or remote trekking)
- COVID-19 (up to date)

**Sun & Heat:** ⚠️ HIGH RISK
- UV Index regularly 10–12+ (extreme). Apply SPF 50 every 2 hours.
- Heat exhaustion: stay hydrated (2–3L water/day), rest 11am–3pm.
- Heat stroke symptoms: confusion, no sweating when hot — medical emergency.

**Animal Hazards:**
- Rabies risk from monkeys, dogs, bats. DO NOT touch or feed animals.
- If bitten: wash wound with soap and water for 15 minutes; go to hospital immediately for post-exposure shots.
- Sea: jellyfish (rinse with seawater, not fresh; remove tentacles with card not hands), sea urchins (wear reef shoes), fire coral.

### East Asia (Japan, South Korea, China)

**Water & Food Safety:** 🟢 LOW RISK (Japan, Korea) / 🟡 MODERATE (China)
- Japan and South Korea: tap water safe, extremely high food hygiene.
- China: do not drink tap water in most cities. Bottled or boiled only.

**Health Concerns:**
- Air quality: China and South Korea experience Yellow Dust / PM2.5 events (Mar–May primarily). Download AQI app; wear N95 on high-pollution days.
- Japan: very low risk. Follow Japanese hygiene standards (hand washing stations everywhere).

**Vaccinations:**
- Japan/Korea: routine vaccines only (MMR, tetanus, flu). No special requirements.
- China: Hepatitis A, Typhoid for rural/adventure travel.

---

### South Asia (India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh)

**Water & Food Safety:** ⚠️ VERY HIGH RISK
- NEVER drink tap water. Sealed bottled water only.
- Avoid salads, raw vegetables, unpeeled fruit from unknown sources.
- "Delhi Belly" affects majority of visitors — have anti-diarrhea medication ready.
- Oral rehydration salts essential.

**Vaccinations Recommended:**
- Hepatitis A (essential)
- Typhoid (essential)
- Hepatitis B
- Rabies pre-exposure (Nepal: stray dogs common; trekking)
- Japanese Encephalitis (rural India / Nepal)
- Malaria prophylaxis (rural areas, low season. Not needed for major cities)

**Altitude (Nepal, Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh):**
- Altitude sickness starts above 2500m. Acclimatize gradually (gain max 500m/day above 3000m).
- Symptoms: headache, nausea, fatigue. If worsening: descend immediately.
- Diamox (acetazolamide) can help — requires prescription; discuss with doctor.
- HACE/HAPE (severe altitude sickness): medical emergency — descend immediately, evacuate.

---

### Middle East (UAE, Jordan, Morocco, Saudi Arabia, Egypt)

**Water & Food Safety:**
- UAE: tap water technically safe but use bottled for drinking.
- Jordan, Morocco, Egypt: bottled water essential.
- Restaurant food generally safe at tourist-facing establishments.

**Health Concerns:**
- Extreme heat (UAE in summer: 40–50°C). Heatstroke risk. Limit outdoor activity during peak heat.
- MERS-CoV: low risk; avoid camels and camel products if concerned.
- Sun exposure: UV very high; SPF 50+, head covering.

**Vaccinations:**
- Routine vaccines. Hepatitis A for Jordan, Morocco, Egypt.
- Typhoid for Morocco and Egypt if eating adventurously.

---

### Europe (Western & Eastern)

**Water & Food Safety:** 🟢 VERY LOW RISK
- Tap water safe throughout EU and UK.
- Food hygiene standards very high.
- Exception: some rural areas in Eastern Europe/Balkans — bottled water preferred.

**Health Concerns:**
- Travel thrombosis (DVT): on long-haul flights, walk every 2 hours, stay hydrated, compression socks.
- Tick-borne encephalitis: if hiking in forested areas of Central/Eastern Europe (May–Oct). Vaccine available.
- Sun: UV high in summer, especially Southern Europe. SPF essential.

**Vaccinations:**
- Routine vaccines only for Western Europe.
- Tick-borne encephalitis vaccine if hiking in Austria, Czech Republic, Baltic states, Alpine regions.

---

### Americas (USA, Mexico, Central & South America)

**USA:**
- Water safe. Food hygiene very high.
- Medical costs are extremely high — travel insurance is non-negotiable.
- Natural disasters: check area for hurricane (Atlantic coast, Jun–Nov), earthquake, tornado, wildfire risk.

**Mexico & Central America:**
- Water: do not drink tap water except in very upscale hotels that state purified water.
- Montezuma's Revenge (traveler's diarrhea): common. Carry Imodium + ORS.
- Zika: present in some areas; consult doctor if pregnant.
- Malaria: rural areas only. Most tourist destinations are low/no risk.
- Vaccinations: Hepatitis A, Typhoid. Rabies if interacting with animals.

**South America:**
- Yellow Fever: required vaccination to enter some countries (Brazil, Peru) — certificate needed.
- Malaria: Amazon region — prophylaxis required.
- Altitude: Cusco, Bolivia, Quito — see altitude section under South Asia.
- Zika: present throughout.

## SAFETY ALERTS BY TRIP TYPE

### Beach Safety
- **Rip currents:** Only swim between the flags. If caught in a rip, swim PARALLEL to shore, not against the current. Signal for help.
- **Sun safety:** Reapply sunscreen every 2 hours, more often after swimming. UV reflects off water — shade doesn't fully protect.
- **Water sports:** Always wear life jacket for jet ski, kayak, paddleboard. Check for strong currents before entering water.
- **Night swimming:** Never swim alone at night or intoxicated.
- **Valuables on beach:** Never leave bag, phone, or wallet unattended. Use a buddy system.

### Mountain / Trekking Safety
- **Tell someone your route:** Leave your exact route and expected return time with hostel/hotel or a contact.
- **Weather changes fast:** Pack rain gear even if forecast is sunny. Afternoon thunderstorms common in mountains.
- **Turn back if:** Weather deteriorates, injury occurs, you're off-pace and sunset is approaching. No summit is worth a night out in the cold.
- **Navigation:** Download offline maps before leaving (maps.me, OSMand). Phone GPS works without data.
- **Wildlife:** Make noise on trail (bear bells or talking) to avoid surprising animals. Store food properly.
- **Altitude rule:** Don't ascend more than 500m per day above 3000m. Rest day every 3 days.

### City Safety
- **Pickpocketing:** High risk in crowded tourist areas (metro, markets, famous landmarks). Keep valuables in front pockets or anti-theft bag. Phone in pocket, not hand.
- **Transport scams:** Use metered taxis or Grab/Uber. Agree on price BEFORE getting into unofficial taxis.
- **Night safety:** Stick to well-lit streets and known areas. Avoid walking alone late at night.
- **ATM safety:** Use ATMs inside banks or shopping malls (not on street). Cover keypad when entering PIN.
- **Scam awareness:** "Friendly stranger" offering tea/coffee at their shop, "closed today" redirects, fake police asking to see your wallet.

### Family with Young Children Safety
- **ID wristbands:** Write parent's phone number on child's wrist with permanent marker or use wristbands at all times in crowded places.
- **Meeting point:** Agree on a meeting point at each attraction in case anyone gets separated.
- **Sun protection:** Children burn faster than adults. SPF 50 every 90 minutes.
- **Food allergies:** Carry a translated allergy card in the local language.
- **Car seats:** If renting a car, bring your own car seat — not always available locally.
- **Water safety:** Children drown silently. Watch continuously at pools and beaches; no exceptions.

## CULTURAL NORMS TO RESPECT

### Universal Respect Rules
- Research your destination's basic cultural norms before arriving
- If unsure: observe what locals do before acting
- Never laugh at or photograph religious ceremonies without permission
- Ask before photographing people — especially women in conservative countries

### Southeast Asia
- Remove shoes at temples and many restaurants/homes (look for a shoe pile at entrance)
- Don't point feet at people, sacred images, or Buddha statues (feet = lowest, spiritually)
- Speak and behave calmly — losing your temper ("losing face") damages relationships permanently
- Monks: women should not touch or hand things directly to monks

### Japan / East Asia
- No eating or drinking while walking (except at festivals)
- Bow when thanking — the deeper, the more grateful
- No tipping (it can be seen as rude — Japan especially)
- Queue strictly — never jump a queue

### Middle East / Islamic Countries
- Dress code: both genders cover shoulders and knees in public
- Public displays of affection are illegal in some countries (UAE, Jordan)
- During Ramadan: don't eat, drink, or smoke in public during daylight hours
- Alcohol: illegal or very restricted in Saudi Arabia, Iran; available in licensed venues in UAE, Jordan
- Photography: never photograph military installations, government buildings, or people without asking
