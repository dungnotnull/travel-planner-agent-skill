## BASE LIST — Required for Every Trip

### Documents & Money
- [ESSENTIAL] Valid passport (check expiry — must be valid 6+ months beyond travel date)
- [ESSENTIAL] Printed + digital copies of all bookings (flights, hotel, tours)
- [ESSENTIAL] Travel insurance policy document and emergency hotline number
- [ESSENTIAL] Local currency (minimum $50 equivalent cash on arrival)
- [ESSENTIAL] Primary payment card (notify bank of travel dates before leaving)
- [RECOMMENDED] Second backup payment card (different bank/network)
- [RECOMMENDED] Emergency contact list (printed, in wallet AND in phone)
- [OPTIONAL] International driving license (if renting a vehicle)

### Clothing
- [ESSENTIAL] Underwear × (days + 2)
- [ESSENTIAL] Socks × (days + 2) — including 1 pair per activity type
- [ESSENTIAL] T-shirts / tops × days
- [ESSENTIAL] Comfortable walking shoes (broken in before trip)
- [RECOMMENDED] Light jacket or hoodie (planes and hotels are cold)
- [RECOMMENDED] One "smart casual" outfit for nicer dinners or events

### Toiletries & Medication
- [ESSENTIAL] Prescription medications × (days + 3 extra supply)
- [ESSENTIAL] Doctor's letter for controlled medications
- [ESSENTIAL] Basic first aid: bandages, antiseptic wipes, blister plasters
- [ESSENTIAL] Pain relief (ibuprofen / paracetamol)
- [ESSENTIAL] Anti-diarrhea tablets (Imodium)
- [ESSENTIAL] Oral rehydration salts
- [RECOMMENDED] Antihistamines (allergies + insect bites)
- [RECOMMENDED] Motion sickness tablets (if flying or boat trips)
- [RECOMMENDED] Toothbrush, toothpaste, floss
- [OPTIONAL] Travel-sized toiletries if flying carry-on only

### Electronics
- [ESSENTIAL] Phone charger + cable
- [ESSENTIAL] Universal power adapter (check destination voltage: 110V vs 220V)
- [RECOMMENDED] Portable power bank (10,000mAh minimum)
- [RECOMMENDED] Earphones or earbuds
- [OPTIONAL] Camera (if phone camera not sufficient)
- [OPTIONAL] Laptop or tablet (if working or extended trip)

### Safety & Emergency
- [ESSENTIAL] Copies of passport and visa stored separately from originals
- [ESSENTIAL] Embassy phone number for your home country (saved offline)
- [RECOMMENDED] Personal alarm or whistle (solo travelers especially)
- [RECOMMENDED] Small padlock (for hostel lockers or luggage)

## TRIP TYPE ADDITIONS

### Beach / Resort
- [ESSENTIAL] Swimwear × 2 (so one can dry)
- [ESSENTIAL] SPF 50+ reef-safe sunscreen (large bottle)
- [ESSENTIAL] After-sun lotion or aloe vera gel
- [ESSENTIAL] Flip-flops / sandals (comfortable, grip sole)
- [ESSENTIAL] Rashguard or UV protective swimwear (especially for kids)
- [RECOMMENDED] Waterproof phone case or dry bag
- [RECOMMENDED] Snorkeling mask (better fit than rental)
- [RECOMMENDED] Wide-brim sun hat
- [RECOMMENDED] Polarized sunglasses (UV400 minimum)
- [RECOMMENDED] Beach bag (lightweight tote)
- [RECOMMENDED] Underwater camera or waterproof action cam
- [OPTIONAL] Fins for snorkeling
- [OPTIONAL] Travel beach towel (microfiber, quick-dry)

### Mountain / Trekking
- [ESSENTIAL] Proper hiking boots (waterproof, ankle support, broken in)
- [ESSENTIAL] Moisture-wicking hiking socks × (days + 3)
- [ESSENTIAL] Trekking poles (collapsible)
- [ESSENTIAL] Daypack / hiking backpack (30–50L depending on duration)
- [ESSENTIAL] Rain poncho or waterproof jacket
- [ESSENTIAL] Navigation: offline maps (maps.me or OSMand), compass
- [ESSENTIAL] Headlamp + spare batteries
- [ESSENTIAL] Emergency whistle (bright color)
- [ESSENTIAL] Sun protection: SPF 50, sunglasses, sun hat
- [ESSENTIAL] Altitude sickness medication if going above 2500m (Diamox — prescriptio needed)
- [RECOMMENDED] Hydration system (2L reservoir or water bottles)
- [RECOMMENDED] Water purification tablets or filter (e.g. Sawyer Squeeze)
- [RECOMMENDED] High-energy trail snacks
- [RECOMMENDED] Thermal base layer
- [RECOMMENDED] Fleece mid-layer
- [RECOMMENDED] Trekking trousers (quick-dry, zip-off)
- [RECOMMENDED] Gaiters (for muddy or snowy trails)
- [RECOMMENDED] Blister prevention (Body Glide or zinc oxide tape)
- [RECOMMENDED] Emergency bivvy / space blanket
- [OPTIONAL] Satellite communicator (Garmin inReach) for remote treks

### City Break
- [ESSENTIAL] Comfortable walking shoes / sneakers (expect 10,000+ steps/day)
- [ESSENTIAL] Small crossbody or anti-theft bag
- [RECOMMENDED] Smart casual outfit × 2 (for restaurants, museums)
- [RECOMMENDED] Compact umbrella (cities rarely have shelter)
- [RECOMMENDED] Reusable water bottle
- [RECOMMENDED] City transit card or contactless payment setup
- [OPTIONAL] Collapsible day bag (for shopping days)
- [OPTIONAL] Dress shoes if fine dining planned

### Camping / Glamping
- [ESSENTIAL] Sleeping bag rated to expected night temperature
- [ESSENTIAL] Sleeping mat or pad (if not glamping)
- [ESSENTIAL] Tent (and practice setting it up before leaving)
- [ESSENTIAL] Camp stove + fuel + cookware set
- [ESSENTIAL] Food storage bags / bear canister (destination dependent)
- [ESSENTIAL] Headlamp × (one per person)
- [ESSENTIAL] DEET insect repellent spray or lotion
- [ESSENTIAL] Camping knife / multi-tool
- [RECOMMENDED] Biodegradable soap and toilet paper
- [RECOMMENDED] Trowel (for cat-hole sanitation in backcountry)
- [RECOMMENDED] Waterproof stuff sacks for gear
- [RECOMMENDED] Firestarter (matches + lighter + tinder)
- [RECOMMENDED] Camp chairs and small foldable table
- [OPTIONAL] Solar lantern (camp ambience + practical)
- [OPTIONAL] Hammock + straps

### Road Trip
- [ESSENTIAL] Driver's license (+ international if required)
- [ESSENTIAL] Vehicle insurance documents
- [ESSENTIAL] Roadside emergency kit: jumper cables, triangle reflector, first aid
- [ESSENTIAL] Physical or offline map backup (GPS can fail)
- [ESSENTIAL] Car phone mount
- [ESSENTIAL] Charging cables and car charger
- [RECOMMENDED] Cooler box with snacks and drinks
- [RECOMMENDED] Reusable bags (for grocery stops)
- [RECOMMENDED] Neck pillow and blanket (for passengers)
- [RECOMMENDED] Playlist / podcast queue prepared offline
- [OPTIONAL] Roof box or trunk organizer (for longer trips)

### Cultural / Heritage (Temples, Mosques, Religious Sites)
- [ESSENTIAL] Modest clothing: shoulders and knees covered at all times
- [ESSENTIAL] Lightweight scarf or sarong (for quick cover-up at sites)
- [ESSENTIAL] Comfortable flat shoes (slip-on for easy removal at temples)
- [RECOMMENDED] Small backpack (large bags often prohibited inside)
- [RECOMMENDED] Guidebook or offline cultural reference
- [OPTIONAL] Binoculars (for architecture detail at large sites)

### Adventure / Sports
- [ESSENTIAL] Activity-specific gear (diving: mask + fins; surfing: rash guard; etc.)
- [ESSENTIAL] Sports-grade sunscreen (sweat-resistant, SPF 50+)
- [ESSENTIAL] Appropriate footwear for activity
- [ESSENTIAL] Helmet (if cycling, via ferrata, rock climbing)
- [RECOMMENDED] GoPro or action camera with mounts
- [RECOMMENDED] Dry bag for electronics near water
- [RECOMMENDED] Quick-dry clothing
- [OPTIONAL] Personal safety equipment (helmet, harness) vs renting locally

## SEASON ADDITIONS

### Hot / Tropical
- [ESSENTIAL] High-SPF sunscreen (pack more than you think — expensive locally)
- [ESSENTIAL] Light, breathable clothing (linen, technical fabrics — not cotton)
- [ESSENTIAL] Insect repellent (DEET 20%+ or Picaridin for dengue/malaria zones)
- [RECOMMENDED] Cooling towel
- [RECOMMENDED] Electrolyte tablets or powder (heat exhaustion prevention)
- [RECOMMENDED] Portable mini fan

### Rainy Season / Wet
- [ESSENTIAL] Waterproof jacket (not just a light layer — actual rain protection)
- [ESSENTIAL] Waterproof sandals or shoes
- [ESSENTIAL] Waterproof bag cover or dry bag for backpack
- [RECOMMENDED] Quick-dry everything (clothes dry overnight)
- [RECOMMENDED] Plastic bags to protect electronics in bag

### Cold / Winter
- [ESSENTIAL] Thermal base layer (top and bottom)
- [ESSENTIAL] Insulated mid-layer (down or fleece jacket)
- [ESSENTIAL] Windproof / waterproof outer shell
- [ESSENTIAL] Warm hat, gloves, scarf
- [ESSENTIAL] Waterproof insulated boots
- [RECOMMENDED] Wool or thermal socks × (days + 3)
- [RECOMMENDED] Hand warmers (chemical, reusable)
- [RECOMMENDED] Lip balm with SPF (cold air + sun = chapped lips)
- [OPTIONAL] Ski/snow gear (if winter sports planned)

## GROUP ADDITIONS

### Family with Young Children (< 12)
- [ESSENTIAL] Child's prescription medications + written dosage instructions
- [ESSENTIAL] Children's pain relief (correct weight-based dosage)
- [ESSENTIAL] Baby/child sunscreen (SPF 50+, zinc-based)
- [ESSENTIAL] Child ID wristbands with phone number (for crowded areas)
- [ESSENTIAL] Portable child carrier or stroller (check airline rules)
- [ESSENTIAL] Familiar snacks from home (for fussy eaters)
- [RECOMMENDED] Children's travel pillow and blanket
- [RECOMMENDED] Entertainment: downloaded shows/games for transport
- [RECOMMENDED] Wet wipes × 3 packs (always more than you think)
- [RECOMMENDED] Changing mat (compact, foldable)
- [OPTIONAL] Travel potty (for toddlers)
- [OPTIONAL] Baby monitor (if staying in separate rooms)

### Large Group (10+ people)
- [RECOMMENDED] [1 per group] Portable Bluetooth speaker
- [RECOMMENDED] [1 per group] Group first aid kit (larger than individual)
- [RECOMMENDED] [1 per group] Power strip with USB ports (shared charging station)
- [RECOMMENDED] [1 per group] Printed group emergency contact list
- [OPTIONAL] [1 per group] Walkie-talkies (for areas with poor signal)

### Solo Traveler
- [ESSENTIAL] Personal alarm or safety whistle
- [ESSENTIAL] Door stopper alarm (for hostel or budget hotel security)
- [RECOMMENDED] Tracking app shared with trusted contact at home
- [RECOMMENDED] Offline entertainment (books, downloaded content)

## DESTINATION-SPECIFIC ADDITIONS

### Southeast Asia (Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, etc.)
- [ESSENTIAL] Mosquito repellent (DEET 20%+ — dengue is real)
- [ESSENTIAL] Plug adapter (Type A/C for most countries)
- [ESSENTIAL] Anti-diarrhea tablets (food hygiene varies)
- [RECOMMENDED] Reef-safe sunscreen (marine park rules)
- [RECOMMENDED] Lightweight long-sleeve shirt (temple dress code + sun)
- [RECOMMENDED] Local SIM or eSIM (cheap and easy to get at airport)

### Japan
- [ESSENTIAL] IC card (Suica/Pasmo) — load in advance or at airport
- [ESSENTIAL] Cash (Japan is still heavily cash-based)
- [RECOMMENDED] Pocket Wi-Fi or eSIM (coverage is excellent)
- [RECOMMENDED] Modest clothing for temple visits
- [OPTIONAL] Portable clothes steamer (wrinkle-free for no-shoe-zones)

### Europe (Schengen)
- [ESSENTIAL] Schengen visa (if required for your nationality)
- [ESSENTIAL] European health insurance card (EU citizens) or travel insurance
- [RECOMMENDED] Rail pass or point-to-point tickets booked in advance
- [RECOMMENDED] Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones are tough)

### Middle East (UAE, Saudi, Jordan, etc.)
- [ESSENTIAL] Modest clothing: shoulders + knees covered for both genders
- [ESSENTIAL] Separate swimwear for public beaches vs pool
- [RECOMMENDED] Respectful head covering for women (required at some sites)
- [RECOMMENDED] Cash for souks and traditional markets

### USA
- [ESSENTIAL] ESTA or tourist visa (non-US citizens)
- [RECOMMENDED] International driving license (vast distances, car rental common)
- [RECOMMENDED] Travel health insurance (US medical costs are extremely high)
- [RECOMMENDED] Tipping guide (15–25% standard in restaurants/taxis)
