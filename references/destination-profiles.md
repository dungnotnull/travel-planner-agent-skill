## SOUTHEAST ASIA

### Thailand
**Climate:** Tropical. Dry Nov–Apr (best); hot Mar–May; Monsoon May–Oct (south stays drier longer).
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Low-Medium. Generally safe for tourists. Petty theft in crowded areas. Scams targeting tourists (tuk-tuk, gem shop, "temple closed today" scams).
**Entry:** Most nationalities get 30-day visa on arrival or e-visa. Passport must be valid 6+ months.
**Cultural:** Remove shoes at temples. Cover shoulders and knees. Buddha imagery on clothing is disrespectful. Wai greeting (palms together). King is revered — never disrespect.
**Health:** Dengue risk (use repellent). Don't drink tap water. Street food is generally safe at busy stalls. Rabies risk from stray dogs — avoid contact.
**Currency:** Thai Baht (THB). ~35 THB/USD. ATMs widely available; 200 THB fee typical. Cash still king outside Bangkok malls.

### Vietnam
**Climate:** 3 regions. North: 4 seasons, cold Dec–Feb. Central: best Oct–Apr. South: dry Nov–Apr.
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Low-Medium. Safe generally. Motorbike snatch theft is real — keep bags on inside. Traffic is chaotic.
**Entry:** E-visa available online. 90-day single entry. Many nationalities get free 45-day visa on arrival.
**Cultural:** Dress modestly at pagodas. Bargain at markets (smile, start at 50%). Remove shoes when entering homes. Pointing with finger is rude.
**Health:** Don't drink tap water. Street food excellent and safe at busy spots. Typhoid vaccination recommended. Japanese Encephalitis vaccine if rural.
**Currency:** Vietnamese Dong (VND). ~25,000 VND/USD. Cash essential outside major hotels. ATM withdrawal limits apply — bring enough cash for rural areas.

### Indonesia (Bali)
**Climate:** Dry season May–Sep (peak); wet season Oct–Apr with heavy afternoon rain.
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Low-Medium. Safe for tourists. Petty theft. Volcanic activity on some islands. Rip currents at surf beaches — respect flags.
**Entry:** Visa on arrival (30 days, extendable) or e-VOA for most nationalities.
**Cultural:** Ubud: respect temple dress codes (sarong required, provided free). Don't step on Canang sari (flower offerings on ground). Hindu customs dominant in Bali.
**Health:** Rabies risk — do not touch monkeys at monkey forest. Don't drink tap water. Bali Belly is common — stick to cooked food and bottled water.
**Currency:** Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). ~16,000 IDR/USD. Cash essential in villages. Card accepted at tourist areas.

## EAST ASIA

### Japan
**Climate:** 4 distinct seasons. Cherry blossom (Mar–Apr) and autumn leaves (Nov) are peak. Summer (Jul–Aug) hot and humid. Winter (Dec–Feb) cold, Hokkaido has snow.
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Very Low. One of the world's safest countries. Almost no violent crime. Natural disaster risk (earthquakes, typhoons in summer).
**Entry:** Most Western nationalities: 90-day visa-free. Some Asian nationalities require visa.
**Cultural:** Queue strictly. No tipping (it can be considered rude). Speak quietly on public transport. Tattoos may restrict onsen access. Bow as greeting.
**Health:** Tap water is excellent (safe to drink). High food hygiene standards. Japanese encephalitis vaccine if rural/rural outdoor activities.
**Currency:** Japanese Yen (JPY). ~150 JPY/USD. Japan is heavily cash-based. Get yen from 7-Eleven ATMs (most reliable for foreign cards). IC card for transit.

### South Korea
**Climate:** 4 seasons. Spring (Apr–May) and fall (Sep–Oct) best. Hot humid summer, cold winter (Seoul below 0°C Dec–Feb).
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Very Low. Extremely safe. North Korea border political tension — limited tourist impact.
**Entry:** Most nationalities: 90-day visa-free.
**Cultural:** Respect for elders (use two hands to receive/give items). Remove shoes when entering homes. Drinking culture is strong — pouring for others, not yourself.
**Health:** Tap water generally safe. Air quality can be poor (Yellow Dust from China, Mar–May) — masks helpful.
**Currency:** Korean Won (KRW). ~1,350 KRW/USD. T-money card for transit. Cards widely accepted.

## SOUTH ASIA

### India
**Climate:** Vast — varies hugely by region. North: best Oct–Mar. South: generally year-round. Monsoon Jun–Sep across most of country.
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Medium. Varies by region. Exercise caution, especially solo female travelers. Petty theft, scams, aggressive touts common in tourist areas.
**Entry:** Most nationalities require e-Visa (apply minimum 4 days before travel). 30 or 60-day options.
**Cultural:** Conservative dress (cover shoulders/knees, especially outside cities). Remove shoes at temples and mosques. Cow is sacred — do not disrespect. Left hand is considered unclean — use right hand to eat/give.
**Health:** Do NOT drink tap water. Typhoid vaccine strongly recommended. Hepatitis A and B. Malaria prophylaxis if rural. Delhi Belly is common — be very careful with food hygiene.
**Currency:** Indian Rupee (INR). ~83 INR/USD. Cash essential. ATMs widespread in cities. Carry small denominations.

## MIDDLE EAST

### UAE (Dubai / Abu Dhabi)
**Climate:** Oct–Apr best (25–35°C). Summer (Jun–Sep) extreme heat (40–50°C) — outdoor activities very limited.
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Very Low. One of the world's safest. Strict laws — drug offences carry severe penalties.
**Entry:** Most nationalities: visa on arrival or visa-free for 30–90 days.
**Cultural:** Modest dress in public (knees and shoulders covered outside malls/resorts). Swimwear only at pools/beaches. No public displays of affection. Ramadan: no eating/drinking in public during daytime.
**Health:** Tap water is technically safe but tastes bad — most drink bottled. Heatstroke risk in summer.
**Currency:** UAE Dirham (AED). Pegged at 3.67 AED/USD. Cards widely accepted. Tipping 10–15% at restaurants.

### Jordan
**Climate:** Best Oct–Apr. Summers hot and dry. Petra: can be cold in winter.
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Low. Very stable and safe for tourists. Petra area: camel/donkey ride touts can be persistent.
**Entry:** Jordan Pass recommended (includes visa + major attractions including Petra). Buy before travel.
**Cultural:** Conservative country. Modest dress outside tourist resorts. Women should cover hair in mosques. Alcohol available in hotels and licensed restaurants.
**Health:** Tap water variable — drink bottled. Petra hiking: bring 3+ liters of water per person — heat exhaustion risk.

## EUROPE

### Western Europe (France, Italy, Spain, Germany, Netherlands)
**Climate:** Generally temperate. Best Apr–Oct. Peak Jul–Aug (crowded, expensive). Christmas markets in Dec.
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Low. Generally very safe. Pickpocketing in tourist areas (Rome, Paris, Barcelona): use anti-theft bags.
**Entry:** Schengen visa for non-EU travelers (90 days within 180-day period). ETIAS required from 2024 (EU equivalent of ESTA).
**Cultural:** Restaurants: dinner is late (8–10pm in Spain, 7–8pm in Italy). Tipping: 5–10% in restaurants (not mandatory like US). Topless sunbathing common in France/Spain (not Germany).
**Health:** Tap water safe throughout. EHIC (European Health Insurance Card) for EU citizens. Travel insurance essential for non-EU.
**Currency:** Euro (EUR). ~0.93 EUR/USD. Cards widely accepted everywhere.

### UK (England, Scotland, Wales)
**Climate:** Mild, changeable. Best Jun–Aug. Rain possible any time. Scotland cold year-round.
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Low. Very safe. Standard urban pickpocketing awareness in London.
**Entry:** ETA required (from 2024) for non-British, non-Irish nationalities (similar to ESTA). €7 per person.
**Cultural:** Queue is sacred — never jump a queue. Tipping: 10–15% in restaurants. "Cheers" means thank you. Pub culture central to social life.
**Currency:** British Pound Sterling (GBP). ~0.79 GBP/USD. Cards accepted everywhere.

## AMERICAS

### USA
**Climate:** Varies hugely by region and season. East coast: 4 seasons. California: mild year-round. Florida: hot and humid, hurricane season Jun–Nov.
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Low-Medium. Varies enormously by city and neighborhood. Research specific area. Gun ownership is legal.
**Entry:** ESTA for VWP countries (apply 72h before, $21). Visa for others.
**Cultural:** Tipping is not optional (15–25% at restaurants, bars, taxis). Sales tax added at checkout. Portion sizes are large.
**Health:** Medical costs are extremely high without insurance. Travel health insurance is not optional. Tap water safe in most cities.
**Currency:** US Dollar (USD). Cards accepted everywhere. Carry some cash for small purchases.

### Mexico
**Climate:** Central highlands: temperate year-round. Coasts: hot, dry Nov–Apr; wet May–Oct (hurricane risk on both coasts Jun–Nov).
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Medium. Varies by region. Tourist areas (Cancun, CDMX tourist zones, Oaxaca, Los Cabos) generally safe. Check travel advisories for specific states.
**Entry:** Most nationalities: visa-free up to 180 days.
**Cultural:** Tipping expected: 10–15% restaurants, 20–25% at tourist areas. Mañana culture — things run on flexible timing. Mezcal and tequila culture strong.
**Health:** Don't drink tap water (even in CDMX). Montezuma's Revenge is common — be careful with raw vegetables and ice from unknown sources.
**Currency:** Mexican Peso (MXN). ~17 MXN/USD. Cash needed outside tourist areas. Avoid airport exchange rates — use ATMs.

## AFRICA

### Morocco
**Climate:** Best Mar–May and Sep–Nov. Summer (Jun–Aug) extremely hot in interior (Marrakech 40°C+). Atlas Mountains: cold at altitude.
**Safety Tier:** 🟡 Low-Medium. Generally safe. Very persistent touts and unofficial guides in medinas. Negotiate everything.
**Entry:** Most nationalities: visa-free for 90 days.
**Cultural:** Conservative Muslim country. Modest dress (cover shoulders/knees) outside coastal resorts. During Ramadan: don't eat/drink/smoke in public during daylight. Homosexuality is illegal.
**Health:** Don't drink tap water. Avoid raw salads and unpeeled fruit from street vendors. Rabies risk from stray cats/dogs.
**Currency:** Moroccan Dirham (MAD). ~10 MAD/USD. Cash essential in medinas. ATMs in cities.

### South Africa
**Climate:** Cape Town: best Nov–Mar (summer). Johannesburg: pleasant year-round. Safari: dry season May–Sep best for wildlife viewing.
**Safety Tier:** 🟠 Medium-High. One of the higher crime rates globally. Take serious precautions: no night walking, no flashy jewelry, car hijacking awareness, "smash-and-grab" from cars.
**Entry:** Most nationalities: 30–90 days visa-free.
**Cultural:** Very diverse multicultural society. Tipping expected (10–15%). Ubuntu philosophy of community. 11 official languages.
**Health:** Malaria risk in Limpopo and KwaZulu-Natal (Kruger area). Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from endemic country. HIV rates high — standard precautions.
**Currency:** South African Rand (ZAR). ~18 ZAR/USD. Cards accepted in cities. Cash needed in townships and rural areas.

## OCEANIA

### Australia
**Climate:** Vast — varies hugely. Sydney: temperate year-round. Queensland: tropical (wet Dec–Mar). Centre: extreme heat. Best overall: Apr–Sep (Southern Hemisphere autumn/winter = mild).
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Very Low. Extremely safe. Wildlife hazards: sun, sharks, jellyfish, snakes, spiders — follow local advice.
**Entry:** eVisitor or ETA for eligible nationalities ($20 AUD). Working Holiday Visa available for under 35s.
**Cultural:** Relaxed and friendly. Tipping not standard (10% appreciated in cities). Outdoor lifestyle dominates.
**Health:** Slip-Slop-Slap: sun protection is critical (UV index very high). Bluebottle jellyfish season: check beach flags. No swimming outside flagged zones.
**Currency:** Australian Dollar (AUD). ~1.55 AUD/USD. Cards widely accepted. ATMs everywhere.

### New Zealand
**Climate:** Temperate. Best Dec–Feb (summer). South Island: colder, dramatic. North Island: warmer.
**Safety Tier:** 🟢 Very Low. One of the world's safest destinations.
**Entry:** NZeTA required for most visa-waiver countries (NZ$23). Australia exempt.
**Cultural:** Māori culture: learn to pronounce place names respectfully. Haka is sacred — don't imitate for fun. Very environmentally conscious — follow leave-no-trace rules.
**Health:** Tap water safe. UV very high — SPF 50 essential. Sandfly bites in South Island: bring strong repellent.
**Currency:** New Zealand Dollar (NZD). ~1.65 NZD/USD. Cards widely accepted.
